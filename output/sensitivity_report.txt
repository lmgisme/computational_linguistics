7. CAVEATS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Category A has only 4 items (Uzbek Persian loans). Small N limits
  the precision of the sensitivity estimate.
- All Category A items are from one language (Uzbek) and one source
  (Persian). Sensitivity may differ for other host/source combinations.
- The automated loan screen identifies structural markers, not confirmed
  loans. Some flagged items may be native Turkic with loan-like features.
- Substrate items may violate Turkic correspondences in different ways
  than known-source loans. A substrate from an unknown language might
  not share the phonological profile of Persian/Arabic borrowing.
- Persian loans in Uzbek are among the most phonologically distinct
  foreign material in the dataset (different consonant inventories,
  clusters, vowel patterns). Substrate items from a typologically
  closer source would be harder to detect.
