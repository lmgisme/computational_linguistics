4. UZBEK MISPLACEMENT
---------------------
Uzbek did not correct into the Kipchak clade when LexStat characters were removed.
Its placement actually degraded — in Task 1 it sits as sister to the entire
Kipchak+Oghuz crown group rather than as an isolated early-diverging lineage as in
Phase 4. This rules out LexStat noise as the cause of the misplacement.

Interpretation: The Uzbek artifact is driven by Persian and Arabic loanword
contamination in the Savelyev-grounded data itself. Heavy borrowing reduces Uzbek's
apparent cognate sharing with other Kipchak languages (Kazakh, Kyrgyz) regardless of
which character set is used. The 64 Savelyev characters include enough loan-affected
entries that Uzbek's Kipchak signal is suppressed at the data level, not the
pipeline level.

Correction would require a loan-filtered Uzbek dataset, which is beyond the scope
of Phase 5. The misplacement is documented as a known, interpretable artifact and
does not affect conclusions about other nodes.


5. OGHUZ CLADE DEGRADATION
---------------------------
The Oghuz clade posterior dropped from 0.526 to 0.4177 when LexStat characters
were removed. This is the expected direction: the 28 LexStat-only characters
contributed marginal positive signal supporting the Oghuz grouping. Their removal
weakens an already poorly-supported deep node.

This does not indicate that the Oghuz clade is wrong — Azerbaijani+Turkish remain
strongly supported as sisters (0.9628) and the three-way Oghuz grouping is
established on independent linguistic grounds. It indicates that 64 Savelyev
characters are insufficient to resolve this node from binary presence/absence data
alone. The Phase 4 92-character run is the better estimate for Oghuz.


6. KIPCHAK CLADE STABILITY
---------------------------
The Kipchak clade (Kazakh+Kyrgyz+Uyghur) posterior held at 1.0 in both runs.
This node is robust to character set choice. The internal Kazakh+Kyrgyz sister
relationship degraded from 0.7556 to 0.3885, consistent with reduced resolution
from fewer characters, but the clade itself is not in question.


7. CONCLUSIONS
--------------
(a) The LexStat-only characters (65-92) do not distort the Phase 4 topology in any
    meaningful way. Including them was the correct decision. The 92-character Phase 4
    tree stands as the primary result.

(b) The Uzbek misplacement is confirmed as a data quality artifact intrinsic to
    Uzbek's heavy Persian/Arabic loan density, not a pipeline artifact. It cannot be
    corrected without loan filtering at the lexical level.

(c) Dropping to 64 characters degrades Oghuz clade resolution and widens the HPD
    interval on tree height, as expected. More characters improve inference; fewer
    degrade it. This motivates Task 2 (NorthEuraLex expansion to 200+ items).

(d) The Proto-Turkic divergence date estimate is stable across both runs (~2008-2012
    BP), consistent with Savelyev & Robbeets (2020) calibration of ~2100 BP. The
    date estimate is not sensitive to the LexStat character inclusion.
