2. CONCEPT FILTERING
--------------------
The following concept categories were excluded from the NorthEuraLex expansion
as they carry no substrate detection value and would inflate the dataset with
predictable borrowing noise (Islamic/Christian calendar, Western numerals):

  - Month names (January-December): 12 concepts
  - Weekday names (Monday-Sunday): 7 concepts
  - Cardinal numbers above ten (11-1000): 14 concepts

All remaining NorthEuraLex concepts were retained, including body parts, nature
terms, animals, basic verbs, adjectives, kinship terms, and basic nouns.
The retained concepts total approximately 983 per language (coverage varies
by language due to missing forms in the source data).


3. DATASET ASYMMETRY — KNOWN LIMITATION
----------------------------------------
Kyrgyz (38 items), Turkmen (39 items), and Uyghur (36 items) remain at
near-Swadesh coverage while the six NorthEuraLex languages are at 998-1,279
items. This ~25:1 coverage ratio will cause LexStat to treat the three thin
languages as artificially divergent in Task 3, analogous to the Uzbek
phylogenetic misplacement in Phase 4 (caused by a different mechanism but
the same structural problem: thin or contaminated coverage distorts distance
estimates).

Mitigation strategy for Task 3:
  - Run LexStat on the full 6,966-row dataset (more data improves cognate
    detection for all languages, including the thin three)
  - Restrict anomaly detection threshold calibration to the six well-covered
    languages only (Kyrgyz/Turkmen/Uyghur anomaly rates will be unreliable)
  - Flag all Kyrgyz/Turkmen/Uyghur anomaly candidates as low-confidence
    in the output
